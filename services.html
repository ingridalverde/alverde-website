Questions

What families ask before they begin.

Is this therapy?

No. Coaching is not therapy and does not treat mental health conditions. Coaching is forward-focused — we work on strengths, goals, habits, and the family relationship. If your family is working with a therapist or psychiatrist, coaching complements that work beautifully.

My child is resistant to coaching. Will this work?

Resistance is common — and understandable. Many young people with ADHD have been told what's wrong with them for years. Alverde Coaching doesn't start with what's broken. It starts with what's working, and builds from there. Most resistant clients become willing participants within the first session.

Do you work with parents, the young person, or both?

Both. Depending on the engagement, we work with the young person directly, with parents separately, or together as a family. The Roots and Flourish packages include parent partnership as a core part of the work.

What ages do you work with?

Primarily teens and young adults — roughly ages 13 through 25 — and their families. This includes high school students navigating academic challenges, college students managing independence for the first time, and young adults figuring out career and life.

Where are sessions held?

All sessions are online via video call. Alverde Coaching is based in Petaluma, California, and serves families nationally.

Where do I start if I'm not sure which package is right?

Start with a Jumpstart session. It's designed exactly for that — to give you clarity about where you are, what you need, and whether coaching is the right fit. Many families move into a longer engagement after their Jumpstart.

Not sure where to start?

A Jumpstart session is one conversation. No long-term commitment.
